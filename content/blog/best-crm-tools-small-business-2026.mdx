# Best CRM Tools for Small Business in 2026: 7 Options Tested & Ranked

Choosing the wrong CRM is an expensive mistake. You either end up paying for features you'll never use, or you outgrow a system the moment your pipeline gets serious. We tested 12 CRM platforms over three months with real sales data.

Here's what actually works.

## 1. HubSpot CRM — Best Free CRM and All-in-One Platform

HubSpot's free CRM is genuinely the best free option on the market — not a crippled teaser, but a fully functional CRM with unlimited contacts, a deal pipeline, email tracking, meeting scheduling, and live chat.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Free tier has unlimited contacts and deals (no time limit)
- Connects to HubSpot's Marketing, Sales, and Service Hubs for a complete GTM stack
- AI tools: ChatSpot for natural language CRM queries, AI email writer
- 1,000+ app integrations via the marketplace

**Where it struggles:**
- Professional tier ($1,600/mo) is a significant jump from Starter ($20/mo)
- Sales sequences and advanced automation require paid plans
- Can feel like overkill for simple pipeline management

**Best for:** Growing B2B companies, SaaS startups, teams wanting marketing + sales in one platform.

**Pricing:** Free forever → Starter $20/mo → Professional $1,600/mo → Enterprise $5,000/mo

---

## 2. Pipedrive — Best CRM for Sales Teams

Pipedrive is built by salespeople for salespeople. The visual pipeline is intuitive, the mobile app is excellent, and the focus on deal progression (rather than contact management) matches how most sales reps actually work.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Visual Kanban-style pipeline with drag-and-drop
- Activity-based selling methodology built into the UI
- AI sales assistant provides personalized coaching
- Excellent mobile app for field sales
- Clean, uncluttered interface with low learning curve

**Where it struggles:**
- No free plan (14-day trial only)
- Marketing features require separate Campaigns add-on
- Customer support features not included natively

**Best for:** SMBs with a dedicated sales process, field sales teams, agencies tracking project pipelines.

**Pricing:** Essential $14.90/user/mo → Advanced $27.90 → Professional $49.90 → Power $64.90 → Enterprise $99

---

## 3. Close CRM — Best for Inside Sales Teams

Close is purpose-built for inside sales — teams that prospect via phone and email rather than field sales or inbound marketing. Its built-in calling, email sequencing, and SMS features mean inside sales reps never have to leave the CRM.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Built-in power dialer, predictive dialer, and call coaching
- Email sequences with A/B testing and open tracking
- SMS and email from within the CRM
- Lead scoring and smart views for prioritization
- Best-in-class reporting for outbound sales metrics

**Where it struggles:**
- More expensive than alternatives for small teams
- Overkill if you don't do high-volume outbound
- Less marketing-oriented than HubSpot

**Best for:** SaaS sales teams, inside sales operations, SDR/BDR teams doing high-volume outbound.

**Pricing:** Startup $49/mo (1 user) → Professional $299/mo (3 users) → Enterprise $699/mo (5 users)

---

## 4. Freshsales — Best Budget CRM with AI

Freshsales from Freshworks offers a surprisingly complete CRM at a lower price point, with Freddy AI providing lead scoring, deal insights, and next-best-action recommendations.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Free plan for up to 3 users (genuinely functional)
- Freddy AI provides lead scoring without manual configuration
- Built-in phone, email, and chat in all plans
- Clean interface that's easy to onboard quickly
- Strong workflow automation on Growth and Pro plans

**Where it struggles:**
- Less ecosystem depth than HubSpot
- Reporting can feel limited on lower tiers
- Customer support quality can vary by region

**Best for:** Teams needing AI-powered CRM without enterprise pricing, startups looking for HubSpot alternative.

**Pricing:** Free (3 users) → Growth $15/user/mo → Pro $39/user/mo → Enterprise $69/user/mo

---

## 5. Zoho CRM — Best Value Feature Set

Zoho CRM offers the most features per dollar of any CRM platform. With AI assistant Zia, sales forecasting, gamification, and territory management, it punches well above its price point.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Zia AI for anomaly detection, predictions, and automation suggestions
- Canvas visual builder for fully customized CRM layouts
- Deep integration with the rest of the Zoho suite (Books, Desk, Campaigns)
- CommandCenter for customer journey orchestration
- Free plan for up to 3 users with reasonable features

**Where it struggles:**
- The breadth of features creates a steep learning curve
- UI can feel dated compared to Pipedrive or Attio
- Setup and customization requires significant time investment

**Best for:** SMBs already using Zoho suite, international teams, companies needing extensive customization at low cost.

**Pricing:** Free (3 users) → Standard $14/user/mo → Professional $23 → Enterprise $40 → Ultimate $52

---

## 6. Monday CRM — Best for Teams Using Monday.com

If your team already uses Monday.com for project management, the CRM layer is a natural extension. It brings the familiar Monday interface to deal tracking, contact management, and sales pipelines.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Same interface as Monday Work Management — zero learning curve if team already uses it
- Fully customizable columns and views
- AI automation for lead enrichment and email generation
- Good integration with marketing tools (LinkedIn, Mailchimp, HubSpot Marketing)
- Strong collaboration features for teams managing long sales cycles

**Where it struggles:**
- Not purpose-built for sales — missing some CRM-specific features
- Expensive for larger teams vs. dedicated CRMs
- AI features still catching up to Pipedrive or HubSpot

**Best for:** Teams already on Monday.com, project-based businesses with long sales cycles.

**Pricing:** Basic $12/user/mo → Standard $17 → Pro $28 → Enterprise custom

---

## 7. Attio — Best Modern CRM for B2B Startups

Attio is the newcomer that's taken the startup world by storm. Built on a flexible data model with real-time enrichment and AI-native features, it feels like the CRM designed for how modern B2B companies actually operate.

**What makes it stand out:**
- Real-time data enrichment — contacts auto-populated with company data
- AI research assistant for prospect and account research
- Flexible data model: create custom objects beyond contacts/deals
- Beautiful, modern UI that teams actually want to use
- Email integration with Gmail and Outlook for sync

**Where it struggles:**
- Less mature than established platforms — some enterprise features missing
- Smaller integration ecosystem than HubSpot or Salesforce
- Free plan limited to 3 users and basic features

**Best for:** B2B SaaS startups, technical founders, teams that find traditional CRMs too rigid.

**Pricing:** Free (3 users) → Plus $34/user/mo → Pro $69 → Enterprise custom

## How to Choose the Right CRM

**Choose HubSpot if:** You want an all-in-one platform and the free tier meets your current needs. Plan for the pricing jump as you scale.

**Choose Pipedrive if:** Your team is sales-focused and needs a clean, visual pipeline without the complexity of marketing automation.

**Choose Close if:** You run an inside sales team doing high-volume outbound via phone and email sequences.

**Choose Freshsales if:** You need AI-powered CRM features on a tight budget.

**Choose Zoho if:** You're already in the Zoho ecosystem or need maximum features per dollar.

**Choose Attio if:** You're a B2B startup that wants a modern, flexible CRM with real-time enrichment.

---

## Bottom Line

For most small businesses, **HubSpot free** is the right starting point — the risk is zero and it's genuinely capable. If you outgrow the free tier and mainly need sales pipeline management, **Pipedrive** is the cleanest upgrade path. If you're running outbound sales, **Close CRM** has no real competition.

The best CRM is the one your team actually uses. Adoption beats features every time.
