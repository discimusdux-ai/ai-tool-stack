## Project Management: Notion vs ClickUp vs Monday

### Notion — Best for Documentation-Heavy Teams
Notion combines docs, wikis, project boards, and databases in one workspace. It's the Swiss Army knife of productivity — and the free plan is genuinely useful.

- **Free plan:** Unlimited pages for individuals
- **Team plan:** $10/user/month
- **Best for:** Content teams, knowledge management, flexible workflows
- **Skip if:** You need Gantt charts, time tracking, or heavy project management features

### ClickUp — Best for Feature-Rich PM
ClickUp tries to replace every productivity tool — and largely succeeds. It's more complex than Notion but far more powerful for structured project management.

- **Free plan:** Unlimited tasks, members, and docs
- **Paid:** From $7/user/month
- **Best for:** Teams needing sprints, time tracking, goals, and dashboards
- **Skip if:** You want simplicity over features

### Monday.com — Best for Non-Technical Teams
Monday's visual interface makes project management accessible to everyone — no PM certification required. Drag-and-drop boards, color-coded statuses, and simple automations.

- **Free plan:** Up to 2 users
- **Paid:** From $9/seat/month
- **Best for:** Marketing teams, agencies, non-technical workflows
- **Skip if:** You're a dev team wanting GitHub/Jira-style workflows

## CRM & Sales: HubSpot vs Pipedrive

### HubSpot — Best Free CRM
HubSpot's free CRM is the most generous in the industry — unlimited contacts, deal tracking, email tracking, and basic reporting at $0. It's the obvious starting point for any startup.

- **Free plan:** Unlimited contacts, companies, deals, tasks
- **Paid:** From $15/month (Starter) for more features
- **Best for:** Startups that want marketing + sales + support on one platform
- **Skip if:** You only need a simple pipeline (Pipedrive is cleaner)

### Pipedrive — Best for Pure Sales
Pipedrive is laser-focused on the sales pipeline. It's simpler than HubSpot, faster to set up, and better at the one thing it does: helping salespeople close deals.

- **Free trial:** 14 days
- **Paid:** From $14/user/month
- **Best for:** Sales-driven startups, outbound teams, pipeline-focused workflows
- **Skip if:** You need marketing automation or support tools (use HubSpot)

## Design: Figma vs Canva

### Figma — Best for Product Design
Figma is the industry standard for UI/UX design. Real-time collaboration, powerful components, and Dev Mode for handoff. The free plan handles 3 projects.

- **Free plan:** 3 Figma files, 3 FigJam files
- **Paid:** $15/editor/month
- **Best for:** Product teams, UI/UX designers, design systems
- **Skip if:** You only need marketing graphics (use Canva)

### Canva — Best for Marketing Design
Canva lets anyone create professional-looking social posts, presentations, and marketing materials — no design skills required.

- **Free plan:** 250,000+ templates, basic features
- **Paid:** $15/month (Pro) for brand kit, AI features, premium assets
- **Best for:** Social media, presentations, marketing collateral
- **Skip if:** You're designing product interfaces (use Figma)

## AI Tools: The New Must-Haves

### GitHub Copilot — For Development
AI pair programmer that autocompletes code, writes functions from comments, and explains existing code. At $10/month, it saves hours of development time daily.

- **Price:** $10/month (Individual), free for students and open-source
- **ROI:** Most developers report 30-50% productivity gains

### Cursor — For AI-First Development
Cursor takes AI coding further than Copilot — full codebase awareness, multi-file editing, and AI-powered debugging. The IDE replacement for AI-native developers.

- **Price:** Free (limited), $20/month (Pro)
- **Best for:** Full-stack developers, AI-heavy workflows

### Jasper AI — For Marketing Content
AI writing assistant trained on marketing best practices. Generates ad copy, blog posts, email sequences, and social content with brand voice consistency.

- **Price:** From $39/month
- **Best for:** Content marketing teams, ad copywriters

## Customer Support: Freshdesk vs Intercom

### Freshdesk — Best Free Helpdesk
Freshdesk's free plan supports 10 agents with email ticketing, a knowledge base, and automation. It's the clear winner for startups that can't justify $50+/agent/month for Zendesk.

- **Free plan:** Up to 10 agents
- **Paid:** From $15/agent/month
- **Best for:** Email-based support, budget-conscious teams

### Intercom — Best for Product-Led Growth
Intercom combines live chat, product tours, in-app messaging, and a help center. It's expensive but drives activation and retention for SaaS products.

- **Price:** From $39/seat/month
- **Best for:** SaaS products, product-led growth, in-app engagement

## Email Marketing: ConvertKit vs Mailchimp

### ConvertKit — Best for Creators & SaaS
ConvertKit (now Kit) is built for creators and SaaS companies. Clean automation builder, landing pages, and a generous free plan.

- **Free plan:** Up to 1,000 subscribers
- **Paid:** From $25/month
- **Best for:** Newsletter-driven businesses, creators, SaaS onboarding flows

### Mailchimp — Best All-in-One
Mailchimp bundles email, landing pages, social ads, and basic CRM. The free plan is limited but functional for getting started.

- **Free plan:** 500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month
- **Paid:** From $13/month
- **Best for:** E-commerce, small businesses wanting one marketing platform
